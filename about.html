Masindi Nafisa Mbolekwa (b.2002) is a visual artist from Johannesburg. His practice is concerned with pictorial space and the ways in which it is utilized in the creation of a dialogue between metaphysical/abstract and material/representational realms in the canon of painting. It is a poetically loaded engagement with personal and cultural history and the ontology of natal alienation within a postcolony through the consideration of forms of earth as spiritual, and the use of abstract form, colour contrast, bodily gesture and archived knowledges as a language to communicate narrative and myth as well as facilitate emotive experience. Mbolekwa holds an Honours in Fine Art from the University of The Witwatersrand (2023). His first Solo presentation “Feast of Flesh and Prayer” was held at Ellis House Gallery in May 2025. He has participated in various group exhibitions including ‘Screen-In-Time’ (2022) at The Point of Order, ‘Siyabuka’ (2023) at Asisebenze Art Atelier, ‘Crossroads: Tsila Tse Pedi’ (2024) at the Viewing Room Gallery, ‘Figurative Frontiers’ (2024) at RMB Latitudes with Origin Art, Tswela Pele’ (2025) with Kalashnikovv Gallery and Symptom: Invisible Forces (2025) with Bode Gallery. Mbolekwa has works in the Nandos UK Art Collection and Moleskine Foundation Collection.


Statement c.26 This practice is a mythico-poetic engagement with self, an ongoing failure to taxonomize the intersections of soul and void. Drawing from afropessimist theory and existentialist philosophy, I attempt in this work to reflect through the union of painterly formalisms and constructivist symbol-making on personal, familial, spatial and ontological histories as they present in my experience of a socio-spiritual identity. The work is a process of knowing, unknowing and unfolding the ways in which self is given form, and that form maintained within the systems, epistemes and spaces of a contemporary post-colony. There is a poetry of the ineffable in this work. It is an ongoing question, a spiritual investigation into the ontology of my blackness. Floating closer to the intersections of several lines of question than at the ends of any, there is the acknowledgement and continuation of a thought-feeling that is failed utterly by the vocabulary of empiricism and linearity. Here, is a younger expression, a union of process and composition to feel out the course of a question I will never finish asking - for even in the brushstroke and the stain and in-between these canvas threads sits an inherent failure to express truly. In the depths of my soul, in the registers of my spiritual and erotic knowledge (Audre Lorde, 1978) I know that this work is a visual treatise on impasse, it is a flirtation with eternal seeking, infinite failure, surrender to an ‘always almost’. I attempt with each work to explore the relationship that exists between the representational symbol as holder, communicator and jumbler of histories, contexts and canons; and abstract form and texture as driver of experiential engagement, as the page upon which language is encoded and read and misread. Mirrored in this relationship are the conceptual forms that bind to material realities a corresponding ripple in the realm of the metaphysical. As fable reflects lesson, so too, does object reflect void or the lack of form. On the canvas a sort of mapping of the real and abstract occurs; figures and essential shapes fight to claim their place as subject. Two worlds and the crossing between each become – not in themselves, but in their relativity – a language outside of legibility, a dance of liminality, an always-almost, a rolling question. In this work I explore the asymptotic attempt to merge and to map, the futility and necessity of these actions
